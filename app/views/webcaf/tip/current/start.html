Access WebCAF

If you are an independent assurance reviewer, please sign to the Review a WebCAF self-assessment (opens in new tab) service.

If you are a peer reviewer, please sign to the Peer Review a WebCAF self-assessment (opens in new tab) service.

About WebCAF

GovAssure is a 5-stage cyber assurance process used to understand the level of cyber resilience across government.

The WebCAF service is part of GovAssure. You can use it to:

  • complete a stage 3 self-assessment and view submitted self-assessments
  • view your stage 4 Peer Review or Independent Assurance Review Report (IARR)
  • complete a stage 5 Targeted Improvement Plan (TIP) and view submitted TIPs

To view or complete any of these stages, sign in to WebCAF.

For information about completing each stage, go to the GovAssure guidance pages.

The current WebCAF is based on version 3.2 of the National Cyber Security Centre's Cyber Assessment Framework (CAF) (opens in new tab).

Sessiout timeout warning

If you are inactive for 90 minutes during a session, you will be signed out to protect your security. If you have already saved pages, these will be stored securely in WebCAF.

If you are signed out while working on a page, anything you have done will not be stored and you will have to start that page again.

Before you start

To complete a WebCAF stage 3 self-assessment, you must have completed stage 1 and stage 2 of GovAssure. This means you will have:

  • completed your organisation’s scoping document
  • identified all your systems for self-assessment
  • chosen the CAF profile of each system being assessed (Baseline or Enhanced)
  • nominated a GovAssure lead in your organisation, to complete the self-assessment
  • created a repository for your evidence

To complete a WebCAF stage 5 TIP, you must have completed stage 4 of GovAssure. This means you will have had either:

  • a Peer Review with a finalised report
  • an Independent Assurance Review with a finalised report
