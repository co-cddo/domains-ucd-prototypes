Introduction

This report presents the organisation’s independent assurance review of the self-assessment submitted on version 3.2 of the National Cyber Security Centre's Cyber Assurance Framework (CAF) (opens in new tab), providing a structured evaluation of how effectively cyber risks are managed across essential functions. The assessment identifies strengths, areas for improvement, and priority actions required to achieve alignment with the Government’s target CAF profile.

The purpose of this report is twofold:

  • To inform senior leadership and the Board of the organisation’s current cyber maturity, ensuring strategic oversight and informed decision-making; and
  • To demonstrate assurance to regulators and oversight bodies that appropriate governance, controls, and improvement plans are in place to protect critical services.

Findings are organised across:

  • Key recommendations (top-level)
  • Objective-level recommendations
  • Principle-level recommendations
  • Outcome-level summaries and recommendations providing both a high-level strategic view and detailed operational insights.

By implementing and tracking the recommended actions, the organisation will strengthen its resilience, enhance compliance with CAF expectations, and build confidence in its ongoing capability to manage and recover from cyber threats.

About the organisation being reviewed

GDS is a business unit of Cabinet Office. GDS follow the CO governance structures and some of their policies but have flexibility more similar to that of an ALB. They have our own ways-of-working, tools, leadership, and suchlike. They have our own governance structures, and a number of directorates that operate live digital services for the UK public 24/7/365. These include Digital Identity, GOV.UK, and the Digital Service Platforms (Pay, Notify, Forms, etc).

The independent assurance review provides an objective evaluation of the organisation’s self-assessment against the NCSC Cyber Assessment Framework (CAF). It validates the accuracy, consistency, and evidence supporting the reported outcomes and recommendations.

Principle-level recommendations

The principle-level recommendations translate the outcome findings into targeted, actionable improvements aligned with the specific principles of the NCSC Cyber Assessment Framework (CAF). Each recommendation focuses on strengthening governance, protection, detection, and response capabilities in a way that supports the organisation’s essential functions.

These recommendations are intended to guide practical implementation and continuous improvement efforts. They should be reviewed alongside current policies, risk assessments, and control frameworks to ensure alignment with the organisation’s risk appetite and target CAF profile.

Interpreting detailed findings and recommendations

The detailed findings highlight areas where the organisation’s current controls, processes, or governance arrangements align—or fall short—of the target Government CAF profile. Each recommendation is designed to guide practical improvement.

Findings should be reviewed, prioritised, and translated into clear actions that address identified gaps.

Assign a priority level to each action:

  • High priority:Issues that present significant risk to essential functions or where current practices fall materially below the target profile. These require prompt remediation and regular progress review.
  • Low priority:Areas of partial compliance or enhancement opportunities that can be addressed through planned improvements or continuous development activities.
