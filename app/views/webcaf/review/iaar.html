Exectutive summary

The One Login programme has demonstrated a comprehensive understanding of its cyber security posture, demonstrated by the fact that there is little difference between the self-assessment and independent validation. The programme has already identified and planned work to addressed key gaps, showcasing a proactive approach to mitigating potential threats.

Given the relative age of the programme, a strong level of cyber security maturity has been demonstrated, showing a good understanding of the threat and risks they face.

The understanding of risks and threats and implementing cyber security measures is generally meeting good practice considering that the programme has been implemented relatively recently. The programme has strong governance processes and effective risk management processes. Notable strengths include solid documentation and assurance processes.

Key improvement areas identified are focused on enhancing identity and access control, as well as system security and exercising. While contextual awareness is evident, there are instances where good practice may not be fully supported by tangible evidence. The programme's scope is limited to AWS and Github technologies.


Key recommendations

Summary
Given the use of suppliers on the programme, we would prioritise focus on recommendations associated with A4, B2 and B4. This will likely focus on implementing policies or plans already documented.

Detailed recommendations
Key areas of improvement, many of which have planned work that should continue:

  1. Increase visibility and ensure flow down of requirements for subcontractors.
  2. Implement and review privileged access policy and role- based access. Identify, assess and risk manage third party connections, especially those associated with privileged access and actions.
  3. Develop and expand the work on data management to ensure coverage aligned with good practice in the CAF, where possible relying less on manual processes.
